About Default Of Credit Card

How Credit Default Occurs

Credit card default occurs when a cardholder fails to make the required minimum payment on their credit card account within a specific period, typically 30 days past the due date. This failure to make timely payments can lead to various consequences and financial difficulties for the cardholder.

When a credit card account goes into default. Late Payment Fees: The credit card issuer may charge late payment fees for each missed or late payment. Increased Interest Rates: The credit card issuer may raise the interest rate on the account, often to a higher penalty rate. Negative Impact on Credit Score: Defaulting on credit card payments can significantly impact the cardholder's credit score. A lower credit score can make it harder to obtain credit in the future and may result in higher interest rates on loans or credit cards.

You have several options to handle your debt. You can continue to pay it off, try to settle the debt for less than owed or file for bankruptcy. One might argue that a fourth option exists, which is to do nothing. But ignoring the problem will not make it go away. If you are able to evade your creditors until the statute of limitations for debt is reached, you then cannot be successfully sued in court for the debt. But remember, even in this unlikely scenario, you still owe the debt, so you have not in fact handled the debt.
